## 🌍 The World

### The Pact & The Taboo

Writing and drawing are **forbidden by nature itself** — not by law, and not merely by custom, though culture has absorbed the fear into its bones. Long ago, humanity’s technology disturbed the natural world. In response, nature offered a bargain: relinquish writing (the engine of technology), and receive magic in return. The pact holds to this day.

**Enforcement is immediate and absolute.** Anyone who writes — intentionally or by accident — is struck by nature: lightning, sudden illness, attack. Everyone has witnessed it. No one debates it. The fear is not religious doctrine; it is lived, embodied memory.

Because of this, the world has no written language in daily use. There are:

- **No signs** on buildings or stalls
- **No labels** on goods
- **No written records** of any kind
- **No drawings or symbols** in public or private spaces

Instead, people use **memory systems**:

- Colored ribbons on dried herbs (each color communicates a remedy’s use)
- Sequences of tapped wooden blocks (a baker’s rhythm signals fresh bread)
- Wax chips and tokens for trade and tracking
- Rich oral tradition — stories, genealogies, medicinal knowledge passed throat to ear across generations

> *“Everyone finds ways to remember. Civilization means not forgetting.”* — Galen

**The one exception:** Wandering priests write very simple, specific religious inscriptions under strict ritual conditions. They are the only ones permitted to do so.

### Technology & Society

The world sits in a **Victorian/medieval hybrid** — no industrial technology, but not purely primitive either.

- People travel on foot or horseback (bareback — no saddles, no carriages)
- Simple hand tools: knives, wedges; no pulleys
- The village blacksmith is the pinnacle of local technology
- Bakeries have brick ovens. Clothing is sewn. A sundial exists at the temple.
- The city has towers that may be thousands of years old — built in a previous age when people wrote and drew. Their architectural complexity is a source of awe and mystery to villagers.
- Magic exists but is diminished from its ancient peak. The pact is fraying at the edges.

### The City vs. The Village

The city is overwhelming, vibrant, and disorienting to Kaida — the tallest building in her village is the three-story inn; the city has soaring towers she can barely comprehend. The city’s merchants have elaborate, sophisticated memory systems: window displays, color arrangements, rhythm signals. The gap between city and village sophistication is vast.

## 🏘️ The Village

A medium-sized town of several dozen houses clustered around a central square, thinning to farmland, then the steep mountain cliffs beyond. Population roughly 200.

**No decorations anywhere.** Houses are plain beige stone with thatched grass roofs. Streets are unpaved but tidy. Clothing is utilitarian — earth tones, practical cuts, no ornamentation. Everything is built for function.

### Governance

- **Town Council** — general civic matters, trade agreements
- **Women’s Council** — social and family matters; meets regularly; significant influence

### Key Locations

**The Bakery**
Kaida and Mariella’s home and workplace. White-washed walls, old-fashioned brick oven in the corner (faint crackling always present), large wooden table in the center with a plain yellow tablecloth. Shelves lined with sacks of flour, sugar, and ingredients. Countertops cluttered with rolling pins, pastry cutters, whisks. A wooden cabinet of worn, seasoned baking pans. Walls hung only with drying herbs. Warm and homey despite — or because of — its simplicity. A small workroom attached to the bakery serves as Galen’s surgery for emergencies.

**The Market Square**
Pragmatic, no-frills. Stalls made of carefully sanded and stained wood, no ornamentation. Vendors arrange goods using visual systems: stacked produce by color, ribbon-tied herbs, artful arrangements. Goods traded: fresh fruits and vegetables, bolts of plain linen and rough wool, blacksmith tools, leather boots and belts, dairy, eggs, baked goods. The aroma is layered — bread, roasted meat, spices. On market day, neighbors gather and gossip runs freely while Kaida only half-listens.

**The Inn**
Dark weathered wood walls, rough worn flagstone floors. Bar carved from the same dark wood with bottles and tankards on hooks. Firelight. A mix of locals and travelers. Laughter, clinking tankards, occasional song. Where travelers stay and news arrives.

**Galen’s Herb Garden (Mountain)**
Up the mountain — a half-day’s walk north on a winding dirt road through lush fields dotted with wildflowers. Orderly rows of carefully tended medicinal and rare herbs. A stone worktable at the center. Isolated, beautiful, and — by the time of the inciting incident — too quiet.

**The Forest**
Rich sensory environment: rustling leaves, birdsong, animal movement. The air thick with damp soil, wildflowers, pine, wild berries, spicy herbs. Streams with moss-covered rocks. Where Galen finds rare plants and takes Kaida on walks. A place of discovery and quiet comfort.

## 👤 Characters

### Kaida (Protagonist)

- **Age:** Late teens / early adult
- **Appearance:** Olive skin, green eyes (with amber flecks — “xior”), thick black hair, freckles on her nose. Raven hair notable enough to stand out in a crowd.
- **Starting position:** Healer’s daughter; comfortable, respected by association. Works the bakery with her mother.
- **Personality:** Observant. Dry, quiet humor. Emotionally self-contained — prefers solitude and her own internal world. Internally warm but guards it. Loves music (weaves complex internal harmonies, never shared). Sensory and tactile — notices smells, textures, sounds in vivid detail. Not comfortable with village social rituals but is well-liked as a fixture through her baking.
- **Core tension:** Her father’s curiosity and restlessness live in her. Her mother’s rootedness also lives in her. She hasn’t decided which wins.
- **Internal ritual:** Counts steps from the bakery to her room. Hums complex internal symphonies while doing chores to quiet mental noise. The melody she hums before sleep is one she can never remember when she wakes.

> *She liked to imagine herself as a creature of solitude, of wandering thoughts, and vivid imaginings.*

> *Bakery orders, recipes, herbal remedies, her father’s advice all swirled around her brain, each vying for attention.*

### Galen (Father)

- **Role:** Village healer and herbalist. The only healer in the village. Skilled surgeon.
- **Appearance:** Early forties. Rides a bright white mare.
- **Personality:** Warm, beloved, perpetually curious and excited. Always discovering something new. Gentle and playful with Kaida. Supportive without pressure. Makes Kaida’s lavender hand lotion — her last “illusion of dependence.”
- **What he seeds:** He was in the smithy late at night when the explosion occurred. He receives special invitations to teach in the city. There are things he refuses to explain.
- **Voice:** Warm, wondering, a touch distracted. Uses nature metaphors. Conspiratorial excitement when he has news.

> *“Did you see any fairies when you were playing in the garden today?”*

> *“I found a new plant in the forest this morning.” His eyes shone. His voice was a whisper, conspiratorial in its excitement.*

> *“Either way, I’m proud of you, Kaida. You have a good heart and a curious mind. You’ll do great things on any path you choose.”*

### Mariella (Mother)

- **Role:** Runs the bakery. Kaida’s daily companion and emotional anchor.
- **Personality:** Social, warm, deeply rooted. Quick with physical affection. Wise in small ways — tells Kaida not to dwell or she’ll “sour the dough.” Busy, capable, unsentimental. Loves market day.
- **Method:** *Practice.* When asked how she lets go of the hard things she sees as a healer’s wife, that is her answer.
- **Voice:** Warm, direct, practical. Calls Kaida “my joy.”

> *“Welcome back, my joy. How was the market today?”*

> *“You’re worrying about Kell. You’ll sour the dough if you don’t let go of things that are out of your control.”*

### Jaxon

- **Role:** Kaida’s companion and friendly rival at market.
- **Appearance:** Eyes that linger on Kaida’s olive skin, green eyes, thick black hair, and the freckles on her nose.
- **Personality:** Playful, confident, fond. Initiates friendly competitions. Looks at her more than at whatever he’s supposedly admiring.
- **Dynamic:** Light and uncomplicated currently. They share market day stalls side by side — he makes pottery, she bakes. He awarded her his bowl as first prize when she won their contest.

> *“Your bread is amazing, Kaida. I’m not sure how you did it.”*
> *“It’s all in the technique. Maybe I’ll teach you someday.”*

### Aric (The Messenger)

- **Role:** Professional messenger — member of the “Messengers of the Realm.” Delivers spoken messages between villages, memorized word for word.
- **Appearance:** Early thirties. Tall, broad-shouldered, chiseled jaw, piercing blue eyes. Short curly brown hair. Light stubble. Brown tunic, dark pants, well-traveled boots. Leather pouch, wooden staff.
- **Personality:** Takes genuine pride in his craft. Warm with children and ordinary people. Finds meaning in connecting people across distance.
- **Significance:** The messenger class embodies how a world without writing maintains civilization — knowledge, news, and relationship travel through human memory and voice.

> *“I’m just a messenger, really. But I do like to think that the messages I carry can make a difference.”*

### Xavier (City Acquaintance)

- **Role:** Met during Kaida and Galen’s visit to the apothecary’s manor. Seven years Kaida’s senior (~25).
- **Appearance:** Broad shoulders, fine brown suit, sharp tailoring. Bashful grin. Soft baritone voice.
- **Dynamic with Kaida:** Mutual flirtation — she leads, he follows. She sends him on small errands; hides in the garden to watch him look for her; draws him with humming. He names one of the moonflower’s colors “xior” — after her eyes.

> *“Your eyes are the lightest brown I’ve ever seen. Almost amber. But they have these little flecks of green in them that seem to float if I blink. That’s xior. Looks just like your eyes.”*

### Kell (The Blacksmith)

- **Role:** Village blacksmith. Loses his left arm in an explosion at the forge that opens Chapter 1.
- **Personality:** Imposing stature, gentle soul. Loved telling stories to children waiting at the forge. Kaida believes he’d be happy as a teacher.
- **Significance:** His accident forces Kaida into her healer role and plants the first questions about what Galen was doing in the smithy at night.

## 🎨 Prose Style & Voice

### Narrative Texture

Sensory and grounded. Kaida notices smell, sound, texture, and physical sensation before she notices ideas. Descriptions layer multiple senses. The world is felt before it is seen.

> *Spice and sweat gave way to lavender and chamomile, the appley tang hitting Kaida’s nose as her horse nipped at the thin white blossoms.*

> *The clean scent of yeast and berries was crowded out by the acrid stench of burnt flesh and hair.*

> *The familiar background noise of the bakery, a noise that was its own silence, interrupted only by the rustling of her curtains.*

### Kaida’s Interior Life

Rich, running internal commentary — precise, occasionally dry, quietly funny. She notices things and files them without acting immediately. An observer first.

> *She allowed herself a single pang of pity for the man and rushed over to give him a loaf of bread.*

### Dialogue Voices

- **Galen:** Warm, playful, slightly evasive. Uses humor to deflect. Excitement barely contained.
- **Mariella:** Direct, practical, loving. Grounds things in the physical. Calls Kaida “my joy.”
- **Kaida:** Dry, observational, often a step ahead. Comfortable with silence.
- **Jaxon:** Easy, teasing, openly fond. Looks at Kaida more than at the subject at hand.
- **Aric:** Measured, warm, storytelling register. Comfortable with anyone.

### What to Avoid

- Generic fantasy-speak (“Greetings, traveler”)
- Exposition dumps in dialogue
- Characters explaining things both would already know
- Overly dramatic internal monologue — Kaida is understated

## 🔮 Mysteries Seeded in the Text (For Reference)

- **The night they fled the city:** Galen and Kaida left the apothecary’s manor suddenly under circumstances he still refuses to explain. Kaida has fragmentary memories: *“Dire wounds… never seen anything like this before.”*
- **Galen in the smithy at night:** He was present when the explosion injured Kell. Kaida doesn’t dwell on what he was doing there — but the reader should.
- **The stone flower:** A powerful sedative Galen discovers and begins to propagate. Its role in his larger purpose is not yet revealed.
